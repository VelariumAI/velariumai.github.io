VCSE / Correctness Models

VCSE is the verification and reasoning engine for Correctness Models (CMs).

LLMs provide the creative spark.
CMs provide the logical grounding.

What are Correctness Models?

Correctness Models are systems designed to produce outputs that are verifiably correct through explicit reasoning, validation, provenance, and controlled inference rather than probabilistic generation.

VCSE implements this model by encoding, verifying, and reasoning over explicit knowledge directly.

VCSE does not approximate knowledge with probabilistic generation.

Core ideas

  • explicit knowledge
  • structured reasoning
  • verification
  • provenance
  • controlled inference

Why Correctness Models Matter

Modern AI systems often produce answers that sound correct but cannot prove what they know.

Verification before confidence.

VCSE vs LLMs

LLMs

  • probabilistic
  • neural weights
  • strong language ability
  • hallucination risk
  • implicit knowledge

VCSE

  • symbolic
  • explicit claims
  • verifier-centered
  • provenance-backed
  • deterministic where required
  • refuses unsupported claims
  • inspectable knowledge

Conclusion: VCSE is not a replacement for LLMs. It is a complementary engine for reliability and verification in Correctness Model workflows.
